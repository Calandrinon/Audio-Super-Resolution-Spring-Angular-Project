Audio super-resolution

Audio super-resolution refers to the task of reconstructing a downsampled recording back to a configuration similar to the ground-truth high-resolution version. As a result, the super-resolution output is an audio recording of a higher sample rate which misses some of the high frequencies present in the ground truth.
